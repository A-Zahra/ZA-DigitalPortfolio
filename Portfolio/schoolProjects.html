Tree of Emotions

The tree of emotions was a group project that was done in Winter-2020. It was an interactive installation in which students at the University library could share their emotions anonymously by selecting a leaf that aligned with their current emotion and hooking it on the tree.
Through their participation, on the one hand, they would give life to the tree and set a compassionate environment. On the other hand, realizing that they have not been the only person who's been happy or stressed that day, for example, they could feel relieved and satisfied. In addition, they would get a quote related to the emotion they had chosen. This would make them feel like a real conversation, they have been able to share their feelings and at the same time, in return, have received some kind of sympathy or advice.

The Leaves

Every leaf represented an emotion. Each emotion had 32 leaves.
Red = Stressed
Blue = lonely
Brown = Comfortable
Yellow = Happy
Green = Uncomfortable
Purple = Feared
White = Peaceful
Orange = Safe

QR Codes

Each leaf had a QR code. Once the QR Code was scanned, the participants were led to that emotion specific page. The purpose behind these quotes was to give the participant a sense of relief, sympathetic, understood or advised.
Each emotion page had 8 quotes stored in it which would have increased the chance that students taking the same emotion leaf does not receive the same quote.

Location

One of the most important factors which helped our project to succeed and attract students attention was the location that we chose.
Although we couldn't get the permission to place our tree on the first location we found in the library, The second location that was offered to us was a perfect spot. It had great lighting and students could see our installation on their way out of the library.

Day 1 Result

Our initial idea was to keep our installation for a whole week and at the end of every day count the number of leaves hooked to the tree. Surprisingly, our tree got fully covered with the leaves in less than two hours after the installation. Therefore, we changed the plan of data collection from every day to every few hours. On the first day, 189 students participated and shared their feelings.
* 32 students felt stressed
* 34 students felt happy
* 22 students felt peaceful
* 25 students felt feared
* 29 students felt alone
* 5 students felt safe
* 26 students felt uncomfortable
* 16 students felt Comfortable

Day 2 Result

The second part of our project was to interview the participants regarding their experience with our installation. However, we didn't have the chance to execute it. Due to the Coronavirus outbreak, the library had to close and despite the fact that we could take a few photos of our tree, we couldn't collect the data of the results on the second day.

Modular Vest

In this project, we were supposed to choose an object and change its functionality from what it is to somthing totally different. In other words, we should define completely a new concept and purpose for our object.
I chose to work on a vest and turned it into a modular toolkit that could be used for various purposes. It had removable pockets and depending on your needs you could choose which one to be used. The second remarkable feature of this vest was its portability. By folding the vest from the middle and adding its handles, you could carry the vest where ever you want.
